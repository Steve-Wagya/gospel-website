The Need for Salvation

Humanity has always sought answers to life’s deepest questions: Why are we here? What happens after we die? Why is there suffering and sin? The Bible teaches that all people have sinned and fallen short of God's glory (Romans 3:23), and that sin separates us from a holy God.

Salvation is necessary because of this separation. Without it, humanity is lost—spiritually dead and without eternal hope. But the good news, or the Gospel, is that Jesus Christ came to bridge that gap by offering Himself as the perfect sacrifice.

Through Him, we are given not only forgiveness of sins, but a restored relationship with God and the promise of eternal life (John 3:16). This is not earned by works, but received through faith.

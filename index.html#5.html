No Sin Is Too Great for God's Forgiveness

It doesn't matter the kind of sins or mistakes you have committed—God’s forgiveness and acceptance are greater. His mercy extends beyond your past, reaching into your present with love and hope.

The Bible clearly shows this in the story of the prodigal son (Luke 15:11–32), where a wayward child is welcomed back not with punishment, but with open arms and rejoicing. It is a vivid picture of how God receives us when we turn back to Him.

"If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just and will forgive us our sins and purify us from all unrighteousness." —1 John 1:9

The Prodigal Son’s Story
The son strayed, rebelled, and fell. But when he returned in humility, his father ran to meet him. His father declared:

"'This son of mine was dead and is alive again; he was lost and is found!'" —Luke 15:24

Just like the father in the parable, God is waiting for you. He doesn’t shame you—He welcomes, forgives, and restores.

"'Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest.'" —Matthew 11:28

This message is a call to respond. God stands ready, knocking at the door of your heart.

"'Here I am! I stand at the door and knock. If anyone hears my voice and opens the door, I will come in and eat with that person, and they with me.'" —Revelation 3:20

Don’t let your past define you. Let God’s love and mercy restore you.

There Is A Need For Repentance

Repentance is more than feeling sorry—it is a change of direction. Literally, it means to turn back: to turn away from sin and self, and to turn toward God. True repentance is a conscious decision to forsake the old life and embrace a new one found in Christ.

Repentance is inseparable from salvation. The Bible teaches that salvation requires both repentance from sin and faith in Jesus Christ. You cannot cling to sin and truly follow Christ. Repentance opens the door to God's forgiveness and grace.

"If my people, who are called by my name, will humble themselves and pray and seek my face and turn from their wicked ways, then I will hear from heaven, and I will forgive their sin and will heal their land." —2 Chronicles 7:14

This verse reflects God's promise: when we turn back to Him with sincere hearts, He is always ready to receive us. Repentance is not a one-time act, but a lifestyle of turning to God daily, trusting His mercy, and walking in His ways.
